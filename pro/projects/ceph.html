Cephalopod Sidestep

Cephalopod Sidestep was a semester-long project developed by a team of 2. I was responsible for all the art assets, including conceptual designs, character sprites, and environment art.

The project involved creating a streamlined pipeline using Unity and P4V as version control to ensure smooth collaboration. We utilized a burndown chart to lay out each sprint, track the game's progress, and maintain a steady pace.

Pipeline & Process

Our workflow was centered around Unity as the game engine and P4V (Perforce) for version control. This setup enabled efficient file management and collaboration between team members.

The pipeline involved defining clear responsibilities, with me focusing on creating and implementing all art assets. A structured approach, including the use of burndown charts, helped us plan each sprint and track our progress effectively.

Process

The development process included the following key steps:

  • Initial brainstorming and conceptualization of the game's theme and mechanics.
  • Sketching and finalizing conceptual art for the characters and environments.
  • Creating production-ready assets and integrating them into Unity.
  • Maintaining version control with P4V for all project files.
  • Tracking progress with burndown charts and adjusting goals based on sprint reviews.
